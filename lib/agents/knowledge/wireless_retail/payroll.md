# Payroll

## How does the payroll cycle work?
Payroll runs on a **biweekly** cycle (every 2 weeks). The cycle is:
1. The pay period closes (2-week window ends).
2. Each **DM** reviews their team's hours and taps **Lock & Submit Timecards**. This permanently locks the timecards for that period.
3. The **Sales Director** downloads each DM's timecard CSV, reviews it, and taps **Mark Approved** for each DM.
4. Once all DMs are approved, the SD taps **Submit to Owner**.
5. The **Owner** receives an email, push notification, and sees a green banner on the Payroll page confirming payroll is ready to submit to ADP.

## How do I submit my team's timecards? (DMs)
1. Go to **Payroll**.
2. You'll see the most recent closed pay period with your team's hours summary.
3. Review the hours. If anything needs correcting, go to Timecards first.
4. Tap **Lock & Submit Timecards**. This locks all timecards for the period — they can no longer be edited by you.
5. The SD is notified by email and a task is created for them.

**Note:** You can only submit after the pay period closes. You'll see "Period closes [date] — submit your timecards after close" until then.

## Can the SD edit timecards after I submit?
Yes. The Sales Director, Owner, and Developer can still edit timecards even after a DM locks them. The changes are reflected in the payroll data automatically.

## DM Timecards (Hourly DMs)
If you're a DM who is hourly, your personal hours also appear in a **DM Timecards** section on the payroll page. The SD can approve each DM's personal time individually.

**DMs cannot edit their own timecards.** If you need a correction to your own hours, contact the Sales Director.

## How do I download the ADP CSV?
SD, Owner, Ops Manager, and Developer can download the payroll CSV:
1. Go to **Payroll** and scroll to **Download Payroll CSV**.
2. Set the date range (From / To).
3. Tap **Download Full ADP CSV**.

The CSV includes: employee name, username, state, pay period dates, regular hours, and OT hours. Employees are grouped by state for easy filtering.

## DM Approval Status
SD, Owner, and Developer see a **DM Approval Status** table showing which DMs have submitted and which haven't, with employee counts per DM.
